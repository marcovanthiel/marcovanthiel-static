How the race works

  1. 1

    Buy one or more tickets

    Each ticket costs € 5.00 and is linked to one numbered duck. The more tickets, the bigger your chance of a prize.

  2. 2

    We set them adrift

    On Saturday 17 April 2027 at 15:00, all rubber ducks float from the upstream end of the Spiegelwaal towards the finish. The current, the wind and a touch of luck do the rest.

  3. 3

    Win a prize

    Winners are announced straight after the race from the riverside stage, and digitally via this page and by email. The grand prize: a car. Alongside it: a series of weekends in holiday homes at home and abroad, and dozens of other prizes.

Frequently asked questions

How do I know which duck is mine?

Each ticket shows the number of its duck. The same numbers are printed on the ducks themselves — so afterwards you can simply check whether your number is among the winners.

Do I have to be there to win?

No. We call or email the winners on the evening of the race. Being there is great fun though — a free live spectacle on the banks of the Spiegelwaal.

What if my duck gets stuck?

We have safety kayakers on the water who nudge stranded ducks back into the current. All ducks are collected after the race; the Waal stays clean.

How many ducks are you setting adrift?

We're aiming for at least 3,000 numbered ducks — the exact number depends on ticket sales. The more tickets sold, the bigger the race and the takings.

I'd like to sponsor. How?

Great — email badeendjesrace@marcovanthiel.nl. We have packages for donating prizes, name placement on the posters, or sponsoring an entire grandstand at the finish.

Isn't the idea a little... quacky?

Rubber duck races for charity have been organised for years by Rotary and Lions clubs across the Netherlands. Everyone has a smile on, sponsors love joining in, and on an ordinary Saturday afternoon half of Nijmegen suddenly turns up at the Spiegelwaal to cheer on a rubber duck. Service Above Self — with a wink.
